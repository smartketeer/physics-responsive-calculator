1

A light bulb is connected to a 120 V source and draws 0.75 A of current. Find the resistance of the bulb and the power it consumes.

Ohm's Law & Power

Given

  • V = 120 V
  • I = 0.75 A
  • Find: R = ? and P = ?
1

Formula (Resistance)

R = V / I

2

Substitution

R = 120 V / 0.75 A

3

Formula (Power)

P = V × I

4

Substitution

P = 120 V × 0.75 A

Resistance
R = 160 Ω
Power
P = 90 W
2

Two point charges of +4 μC and −6 μC are separated by a distance of 0.03 m. Find the magnitude of the electrostatic force between them.

Coulomb's Law

Given

  • q₁ = +4 × 10⁻⁶ C
  • q₂ = −6 × 10⁻⁶ C
  • r = 0.03 m
  • k = 8.99 × 10⁹ N·m²/C²
  • Find: F = ?
1

Formula

F = k|q₁||q₂| / r²

2

Substitution

F = (8.99×10⁹)(4×10⁻⁶)(6×10⁻⁶) / (0.03)²

3

Numerator

= (8.99×10⁹)(2.4×10⁻¹¹) = 215.76 N·m²

4

Denominator

r² = (0.03)² = 9×10⁻⁴ m²

5

Division

F = 215.76 / 9×10⁻⁴ ≈ 239,733 N ≈ 2.4 × 10⁵ N

Answer
F ≈ 2.40 × 10⁵ N (attractive)
3

A +5 nC point charge creates an electric field. Find the electric field strength at a point 0.20 m away from the charge.

Electric Field

Given

  • Q = +5 × 10⁻⁹ C
  • r = 0.20 m
  • k = 8.99 × 10⁹ N·m²/C²
  • Find: E = ?
1

Formula

E = kQ / r²

2

Substitution

E = (8.99×10⁹)(5×10⁻⁹) / (0.20)²

3

Numerator

= (8.99×10⁹)(5×10⁻⁹) = 44.95 N·m²/C

4

Denominator

r² = (0.20)² = 0.04 m²

5

Result

E = 44.95 / 0.04 = 1123.75 N/C ≈ 1124 N/C

Answer
E ≈ 1124 N/C (directed away from charge)
4

A wire 0.5 m long carries a current of 8 A. It is placed in a magnetic field of 0.3 T at an angle of 60° to the field. Find the magnetic force on the wire.

Magnetic Force (Wire)

Given

  • B = 0.3 T
  • I = 8 A
  • L = 0.5 m
  • θ = 60°
  • Find: F = ?
1

Formula

F = B × I × L × sin(θ)

2

Convert angle

sin(60°) = 0.866

3

Substitution

F = 0.3 × 8 × 0.5 × 0.866

4

Calculation

F = 1.2 × 0.866 = 1.0392 N

Answer
F ≈ 1.04 N
5

A proton (q = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C) moves at 2 × 10⁶ m/s perpendicular to a magnetic field of 0.4 T. Find the magnetic force acting on the proton.

Magnetic Force (Charge)

Given

  • q = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C
  • v = 2 × 10⁶ m/s
  • B = 0.4 T
  • θ = 90° (perpendicular) → sin(90°) = 1
  • Find: F = ?
1

Formula

F = q × v × B × sin(θ)

2

Substitution

F = (1.6×10⁻¹⁹)(2×10⁶)(0.4)(1)

3

Calculation

F = 1.6×10⁻¹⁹ × 8×10⁵ = 1.28×10⁻¹³ N

Answer
F = 1.28 × 10⁻¹³ N
